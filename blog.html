Why does paper turn yellow when left for a long time ?

Julia

16 DEC 2022

34



Have you ever seen old books from the time of your parents or grandparents that have an old yellow color, do you think it is because the printing technology at that time was not as developed as it is now? Yes, but only part of it. Because paper, like humans, is also "aging", so it will age due to environmental impacts and time as well. So do you want to know why that is so? And what will cause this situation the most to find a way to prevent and help preserve printed paper for longer?

Find out the cause of paper yellowing

As you know, the material for making paper is from natural wood. Through the process of grinding, compounding, papermaking and manufacturing the finished product, wood turned into the paper we are using today. The binding of the pulp in the manufacturing process is due to cellulose. Thanks to that, the paper has the toughness to carry out the printing process.


The phenomenon of paper printing books and newspapers yellowing after a period of use is caused by the cellulose in the paper under the influence of oxygen in the air. Just like that, the white color of cellulose will gradually turn yellow over a period of use.
In addition, light is also the cause of accelerating the "aging" process of papers. When the cellulose present in the paper is exposed to light, photosynthesis will occur, so day by day, printed paper, photo paper or newspaper will turn yellow.

Measures are in place to prevent yellowing on paper

In order to prevent direct light and paper documents, people are equipped with multi-colored glasses for bookcases in libraries so that the red, yellow, yellow-orange colors in the light will be absorbed less. This way can reduce the harmful effects of light on the color of printed books and newspapers. As a result, the preservation of books and documents will become better.



In addition, to avoid the impact of environmental conditions, some types of photo printing paper and label paper are coated with a glossy film. This approach helps preserve the durable paper structure over time, helping to preserve better print quality of images and content. In addition, in offices, most papers and documents are preserved in cover templates and document files to avoid damage and yellowing.
